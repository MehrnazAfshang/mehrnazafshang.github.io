We cannot solve our problems with the same thinking we used when we created them. —Albert Einstein

I am currently a research scholar at the Bradley department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Virginia Tech under the supervision of Prof. Dhillon. I received my B.E. degree in Electrical Engineering from Shiraz University of Technology, Iran, in 2011 and my Ph.D. degree from Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, in 2016. During my Ph.D., I was a recipient of the SINGA Fellowship. Since January 2015, I have been a visiting student and later a research scholar at Virginia Tech. My research interests include communications theory, stochastic geometry, device-to-device networks, and wireless ad hoc and heterogeneous cellular networks.
